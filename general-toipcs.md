German questions are primarly divided into 

W-Fragen.
Using question words like

* wer - who
* was - what
* wie - how
* wo - where
* wann - when

Ja/Nein-Fragen (verb-first)



***W-Fragen*** begin with a "W" word, placing the verb in the second position.

***Ja/Nein - Fragen*** start with the verb, to which the answer is "yes" or "no".

Key types of questions in German:

* W-Fragen (Open Questions):
  Use question words to ask specifig information.
    * Wer? (Who?) - Wer ist das? (Who is that)
    * Was? (What?) - Was machst du? (What are you doing?)
    * Wann? (When?) - When beginnt der Film? (When does the film start?)
    * Wo / Wohin / Woher ? (Where?/Where to?/Where from?) - Wo wohnst du? (Where do you live.)
    * Warum? / Wieso? / Weshalb? (Why?) - Warum kommst du so spät? (Why are you comming so late?)
    * Wie? (How? / How much? / How Many?) - Wie geht es dir? ( How are you?)
    * Welcher/Welche/Welches? (Which?) - Welches Buch liest du? (Which book are you reading)

* Ja/Nein-Fragen (Closed Questions):
  These questions start directly with  the conjugated verb.  
    * Kommst du aus Deutschland? (Are you from Germany?)
    * Hast du Hunger? (Are you hungry?)

* Alternative Questions:
  Offer choices, often using "oder"
    * Möchtest du Tee oder Kaffee? (Do you want tee or coffee?A

* Indirect Questions:
  Often used to be polite, starting with phrases like "Können Sie mir sagen...".  
    * Wissen Sie, wo der Bahnhof ist? (Do you know where the station is?)  

Structure Rules:

* W-Fragen: Question word + verb + subject + ..... (Was machst du heute?) 
* Ja/Nein-Fragen: Verb + subject + ... (Gehst du nach Hause?)
