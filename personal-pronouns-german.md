In a sentence in general there will be subject + Object

sub + verb + object

In German whether the object Accusative or Dative is decided by Verb and Preposition.

***Verbs***

1. Nominative
    1. Seine = to be
    2. Heißen = to be called
    3. Werden = Become
    4. Bleiben = Remain

2. Dative
    1. Danken = To thank
    2. Ein Geht = To go
    3. Folgen = Follow
    4. Gehören = Belong
    5. Gratulieren = Congratulate
    6. Gefallen = To like
    7. Helfen = To help
    8. Passen = To Fit
    9. Passieren = Happen
    10. Antworten = To Answer
    11. Schmecken = Taste Good

Remaining words are Akkusative 

Some examples

3. Akkusative
    1. Suchen = to search
    2. lieben = to love
    3. trinken = to drink
    4. treffen = to meet
    5. kaufen = to purchase
    6. bringen = to bring
    7. nehmen = to take
    8. sehen = to see
    9. haben = to have
    10. vergessen = to forget

Lets first look in English.

The 8 Parts of Speech Explained.

1. Noun: Names a person, place, thing, or idea (e.g., dog, city, happiness)
2. Pronoun: Replaces a noun to avoid repetition (e.g., he, she, they it)
3. Verb: Expresses action or state of being (e.g., run, eat, is)
4. Adjective: Describes or modifies a noun or pronoun (e.g. Red, tall, delicious)
5. Adverb: Modifies a verb, adjective, or other adverb (e.g. quickly, very, often).
6. Preposition: Shows relationship, direction, or location (eg., in, on, under, with).
7. Conjunction: Connects words, phrases, or clauses (e.g., and, but, or, because).
8. Interjection: Expresses strong emotion or exclamation ( e.g., Wow! Ouch! Hey!)

----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The 8 Parts of Speech Explained in German.

1. Noun(Nomen / Substantiv / Hauptwort): Refers to persons, places, things, or ideas; always capitalized (e.g., der Hund, die Liebe, Berlin)
2. Pronoun (Pronomen ): Replaces a noun (e.g., ich, du, er, es. sie, wir, ihr. sie/Sie) 
3. Verb (Verb): Expresses action or state of being (e.g., machen, sein, haben, schreiben)
4. Adjective ( Adjektiv): Describes or modifies a noun, changes form based on gender/case (e.g., schön, groß, alt, schnell.
5. Adverb (Adverb): Modifies verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs, telling how, when, or where (e.g., hier, dort, schnell, heute)
6. Preposition (Präposition): Shows relationships, often preceding a noun or pronoun (e.g., in, auf, mit, für, durch)
7. Conjuction(Konjunktion): Joins words, phrases, or clauses (e.g., und, oder, aber, weil)
8. Interjection(Interjektion): Expresses sudden emotion or exclamation (e.g., Oh!, Ach!, Wow!)
