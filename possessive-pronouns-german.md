what is nominative accusative dative and genitive in english?

***Nominative***

The person or thing performing the action.

* Example: ***She*** (nominative) runs
* Pronouns: I, You, he, she, it , we , they

***Accusative***

The person or thing directly receiving the action.

* Example: The Dog chased ***him*** (accusative)
* Pronouns: me, you, him, her, it, us, them

***Dative***

The recepient of the action (Often corresponds to "to" or "for" )

* Example: I gave ***her*** (Dative) the book.

* Usage: In English, this often overlaps with the accusative/objective form (me, him, her, them)

***Genitive (Possessive Case)***

Shows ownership, origin, or relationship.

* Example: That is ***John's*** (Genitive) car.
* Example: That is ***his*** (Genitive) book.
